Observations


  • The users who use an e-musement card (DDR membership card) use more features than those who donâ€™t
    • Users without the card did not explore past the initial selection of songs given or change gameplay settings, while e-musement card users had various customizations
  • Initial loading screen was confusing to new users, they wanted to play with two players but could not figure out why they could not play.

Interview


  1. Have you played this game or similar games before?
  2. Is there anything confusing about the selections you were shown?
  3. Did you play with any goal in mind?
  4. Were you aware of the options menu? If so, how did you learn about it?

  • The users who had e-musement cards have played this arcade cabinet before and play often, while the group without them were first time players.
  • Every user responded that there were no confusions with the interface, even though two new users had difficulty starting the game
    • The new users were also happy with the song choices and did not know they were shown only a small sample of songs to play
  • The users with e-musement cards focused on improvement, in both being able to play harder songs and getting higher scores on songs they already played. The users without a card played for nostalgia and for fun with their partner.
  • The people who knew about the options menu either played a previous version of the game, where it was explicitly shown how to access the menu, or learned by watching someone else open the menu.
